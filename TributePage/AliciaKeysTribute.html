I recognize now that how you look is your statement, because it's a claiming of yourself. There's something so powerful about being my own gorgeous, beautiful, powerful, unique self

Alicia Keys

The year is 2016, my definition of beauty is still limited to heavily made up girls i see on the screen. I admire their seemingly perfect faces, they seem to have perfect bone structure, their faces glow in all the right places, their lips look beautiful even with no lipstick,must be lucky genes. Don't even get me started on their perfect hair, how does a chocolate glazed girl like me end up with such straight, sleek, shiny hair.

The year is 2016, along comes Alicia Keys, Alicia Aguello Cook to be exact.This isn't the Alicia i could recognize, it's all gone, skinny frame, sleek hair, painted lips, it's all gone.Before me is a raw, real, full and beautiful without aid Alicia.

What happens when one person shows us the beauty of embracing who we are, scars, freckles and all?

The straight sleek hair is gone, i now bear a crown of my thick, bouncy, kinky curls. The contoured 'bone-structure' is gone, my God given bones will have to do. The highlighter glow is a thing of the past, baby i glow from the inside and it shows on my face too.

This is who i am, embracing the real because it is true beauty. This is my tribute to Alicia, the one who opened my eyes.
